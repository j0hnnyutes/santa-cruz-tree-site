## Common Tree Concerns in Monterey County

### Monterey Cypress and Monterey Pine

The Monterey cypress (*Cupressus macrocarpa*) and Monterey pine (*Pinus radiata*) are iconic trees of the peninsula — both native to a remarkably small natural range centered on the Monterey Bay. Mature specimens are stunning, but they age and decline, and dead or dying conifers near homes represent serious hazard.

**Pitch canker** (*Fusarium circinatum*) has devastated Monterey pine populations throughout coastal California. Signs include resinous cankers on limbs and trunk, yellowing needles, and dead branch tips. Infected trees generally cannot be saved and should be removed before they become hazard trees.

Monterey cypress is more resistant to pitch canker but is susceptible to **bot canker** and other fungal pathogens in stressed conditions. Trees weakened by drought, root damage, or construction impacts are most vulnerable.

### Coastal Live Oak

Coast live oaks are abundant throughout Monterey County and are among the most ecologically valuable trees in the region. They require oak-specific care protocols — especially around pruning timing and irrigation — to avoid Sudden Oak Death. Any oak work should be performed by someone familiar with current SOD best practices.

### Ornamental Trees in Historic Neighborhoods

Pacific Grove, Carmel, and the older neighborhoods of Monterey contain many large ornamental trees — magnolias, jacarandas, liquidambars, and others — planted in the early 20th century. These trees often have complex canopies that require careful pruning to maintain structure and appearance without over-cutting.

## Tree Removal Permits in Monterey

Tree permit requirements vary across Monterey County's many jurisdictions:

- **City of Monterey:** Requires a Heritage Tree permit for trees of significant size or species. Contact the Public Works and Planning Department for current requirements.
- **Pacific Grove:** Has a protected tree ordinance. Check with city planning before any removal.
- **Carmel-by-the-Sea:** Has significant tree protection ordinances — even trimming some protected trees may require permits.
- **Unincorporated Monterey County:** Governed by county ordinance; requirements depend on parcel type and location.

When we provide estimates in Monterey County, we flag any applicable permit requirements for your specific property and jurisdiction.
