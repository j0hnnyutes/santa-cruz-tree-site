If you're wondering what tree removal costs in Santa Cruz, you're not alone — it's one of the most common questions we get. The honest answer is that pricing varies widely depending on several factors, but this guide will walk you through everything so you know what to expect before you call.

## Average Tree Removal Cost in Santa Cruz, CA

In Santa Cruz County, tree removal typically costs between **$300 and $2,500** for most residential trees, with larger or more complex jobs running higher. Here's a rough breakdown by tree size:

- **Small trees (under 25 feet):** $200–$500
- **Medium trees (25–60 feet):** $500–$1,200
- **Large trees (60–100 feet):** $1,000–$2,500
- **Very large or hazardous trees:** $2,500–$5,000+

These are ballpark ranges. The only way to get an accurate number is an on-site evaluation — any contractor quoting a flat price without seeing the tree should raise a flag.

## What Drives Tree Removal Costs Up

### Tree Height and Trunk Diameter

The bigger the tree, the more labor, equipment, and time required. A 30-foot fruit tree is a half-day job. A 90-foot eucalyptus or redwood can take a full crew two days.

### Location and Access

A tree in an open backyard with good equipment access costs less to remove than one wedged between your house and your neighbor's fence. Limited access means slower, more careful piece-by-piece removal — and that takes time.

### Crane vs. Standard Removal

Many large trees in Santa Cruz County require a crane, especially redwoods or eucalyptus near structures. Crane rentals add cost (typically $500–$800/day), but they're often safer and faster than alternative methods for big removals.

### Number of Trunks and Branch Spread

Multi-stemmed trees or trees with wide, heavy canopies require more cuts, more rigging, and more hauling. That adds up.

### Hazard Level

A dead, leaning, or structurally compromised tree takes more care to remove safely. Working around a house, power line, or fence adds complexity and time — which adds cost.

### Stump Grinding

Most removal quotes don't automatically include stump grinding. Stump grinding typically adds **$75–$300** depending on stump diameter. We recommend grinding stumps: they're trip hazards, they attract pests, and they can sprout suckers for years.

## What's Usually Included in a Tree Removal Quote

A reputable tree service should include:

- All cutting and rigging
- Debris removal and hauling
- Site cleanup
- Wood chipping (brush and smaller branches)

Ask whether the quote includes log rounds (larger trunk sections) — some companies leave those on-site unless you ask for them to be hauled. Firewood-sized rounds can actually be left at your request if you have a fireplace.

## What's Often NOT Included

- Stump grinding (usually quoted separately)
- Permits (see our guide on Santa Cruz tree removal permits)
- Repairs to fences or landscaping damaged during removal

## Why Santa Cruz Tree Removal Can Cost More

Santa Cruz County has some unique factors that affect pricing:

**Eucalyptus.** The county has an enormous number of mature eucalyptus trees — they grow fast, reach enormous heights, and have heavy, unpredictable wood. They're some of the trickier removals in our region.

**Redwoods.** Coastal redwoods are majestic, but removing one near a structure requires experience, proper equipment, and careful planning. Not every tree company has done it at scale.

**Terrain.** Parts of Santa Cruz County — especially the mountains, Boulder Creek, and Felton areas — have steep, challenging terrain that makes access and equipment use more difficult.

**Permits.** Some removals require a city or county permit (more on that in our permit guide). Permit fees and the time required to obtain them can add to overall project cost.

## How to Get an Accurate Quote

The best approach is to get two or three estimates from licensed, insured companies. When comparing quotes:

- Make sure each quote covers the same scope (debris removal, stump grinding or not)
- Verify the contractor is licensed as a CA contractor and carries general liability and workers' comp insurance
- Ask whether they use a certified arborist to assess the job

Don't automatically go with the lowest bid. Tree removal near a structure is not the place to cut corners. An uninsured crew that damages your roof or a neighbor's property leaves you holding the bill.
