The agricultural landscape of South Santa Cruz County and the foothill communities of Santa Clara County — Watsonville, Pajaro, Corralitos, Gilroy, and Morgan Hill — have their own tree care needs that differ from the coastal and mountain communities to the north. Row crops sit alongside large ranches with mature heritage trees; residential neighborhoods have sprawling oaks, mature walnuts, and ornamentals that were planted decades ago and now require professional attention.

Santa Cruz Tree Pros extends service into this region with the same licensed, insured, ISA-certified team that serves all of Santa Cruz County.

## Tree Care Needs in South Santa Cruz and South County

### Heritage and Ranch Trees

Properties in Corralitos, Freedom, and the Pajaro Valley often feature large heritage trees — valley oaks (*Quercus lobata*), blue oaks (*Quercus douglasii*), and massive eucalyptus stands. These trees may have never been professionally maintained, and large, aging specimens with deferred care often require significant structural pruning or removal.

Valley oak in particular deserves careful treatment — they can live for 600 years and are protected by state and local ordinances. Any significant pruning or removal should involve an arborist assessment and permit consultation.

### Fruit and Walnut Orchards

The Pajaro Valley's agricultural character means that property owners often have mature walnut trees, old apple orchards, or fruit trees that need professional-level pruning for health and production. Structural pruning of fruit trees differs from ornamental pruning — it's focused on maintaining scaffold structure, opening the canopy for light and air, and extending the productive life of the tree.

### Residential Trees in Watsonville

Watsonville's older neighborhoods have mature residential trees — liquidambars, Chinese elms, ornamental plums and cherries, and scattered large eucalyptus — that need periodic trimming, structural care, and in some cases removal when decline or hazard conditions develop.

## Tree Services in Watsonville and Gilroy

**Tree removal:** We remove trees of all sizes throughout the South County region, including hazard trees, dead trees, and trees impacting structures or utilities. Crane service is available for large removals.

**Tree trimming and pruning:** Structural pruning for large trees, fruit tree pruning for production properties, routine trimming for residential trees. We follow ANSI A300 standards.

**Emergency tree service:** Storm damage, split trunks, fallen limbs on structures — we respond to urgent situations throughout the region, typically within 24 hours.

**Stump grinding:** Full below-grade stump removal. Particularly important in agricultural settings where stumps in or near cultivated areas create access and equipment problems.

**Arborist reports:** Written assessments for permit applications, real estate transactions, or documentation of tree condition and recommended care.

## Permit Considerations in Watsonville

The City of Watsonville has tree protection requirements for certain heritage trees and trees in specific zoning areas. Before removing any large tree in the city limits, check with the Watsonville Planning Department to determine whether a permit is required.

For Santa Cruz County unincorporated areas (Corralitos, Freedom, Pajaro), the County's tree removal policies apply — species-specific protections and size thresholds determine permit requirements.

In Santa Clara County jurisdictions (Gilroy, Morgan Hill), city ordinances govern tree removal permits. Gilroy's Heritage Tree Ordinance protects trees meeting certain size criteria. We can advise on the applicable requirements during your estimate.

## What Makes South County Tree Work Different

**Terrain and access.** Many properties in Corralitos and the foothills south of Gilroy have steep terrain that limits equipment access. We're experienced with restricted-access removals that require climbing, rigging, and hand work rather than heavy equipment.

**Agricultural sensitivity.** On working ranch and orchard properties, we're careful about protecting surrounding crops and vegetation, managing debris appropriately, and avoiding soil compaction in agricultural areas.

**Valley oak protection.** Valley oaks are one of California's most protected tree species, and their presence on a property adds both value and regulatory complexity. We assess and document valley oak conditions using proper methodology and can help navigate the permit process for any work that requires it.
